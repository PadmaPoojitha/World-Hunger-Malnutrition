World Hunger and Malnutrition

What is Hunger?

World Hunger & Malnutrition have been rising concern. The amount of food produced globally would be sufficient to feed every person on the earth. Yet up to 828 million people remain undernourished. Hunger is a severe food insecurity where people do not eat due to financial, accessibility, or other resources.

Project Analysis

In this project we aim to identify the top 10 countries that are being affected by world hunger and malnutrition. We will explore various factors such us global hunger index, stunting, wasting, undernourishment, and calorie intake to access the countries. The actions been taken in 5 countries will be discussed.

The Percent of Undernourished Population

The countries with highest Undernourished Population were India, China, Pakistan, Nigeria, Bangladesh, Indonesia, Ethiopia, Uganda, Tanzania, Kenya.

Global Hunger Index

The global hunger index measures three aspects of hunger: inadequate food availability, deficiencies in children's nutritional status, and child mortality. India, Nigeria and Uganda seem to have the highest index among the undernourished counties.

Childhood Malnutrition

Childhood malnutrition is a result of persistent or repeated undernutrition, which is frequently correlated with low socioeconomic situations, poor maternal health and nutrition, recurrent illnesses, and/or ineffective baby and young child feeding and care throughout the early years of life. Low height for age is referred to as stunting and low weight-to-height is referred to as wasting. We observed that India, Ethiopia, Pakistan, Nigeria and Tanzania have highest numbers in stunting. While India, Bangladesh, Ethiopia, Nigeria and Pakistan have highest index in wasting. The under weight is high in India, Bangladesh, Indonesia and Pakistan
